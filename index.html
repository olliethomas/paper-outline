Results and Discussion

Notes

  • This sections should be organised according to major topics. The separate parts should have subheadings to make the organisation clear. Try to make these headings as specific and information-rich as possible.

  • In the outline, do not write a significant amount of text, but get all the data in their proper place: Any text should simply indicate what will go in that section.

  • Remember to think of a paper as a collection of experimental results, summarised as clearly and economically as possible in figures, tables, equations, and schemes. The text in the paper serves just to explain the data, and is secondary. The more information can be compressed into tablets, equations, etc., the shorter and more readable the paper will be.

Conclusions

Notes

  • Try to summarise the conclusions of the paper as a list of short phrases or sentences.

  • The conclusion section should be a conclusion, not a summary. It should add a new, higher level of analysis, and should indicate explicitly the significance of the work.

Points of Style

  • Do not use nouns as adjectives.
  • Describe experimental results uniformly in the past tense .
  • Use active voice whenever possible.
  • Complete all comparisons.
